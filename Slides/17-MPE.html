PLP tools: MPE

The Most Probable Explanation (MPE) query is a typical PLP task where the goal is to compute the most probable possible world compatible with the given evidence.
$MPE(evidence(arg(hotel\_X))): \omega = \{bias(quiet),\allowbreak \lnot bias(week),\allowbreak bias(hotel\_X),\allowbreak \lnot bias(anne),\allowbreak \lnot bias(noise),\allowbreak bias(hotel\_Y),\allowbreak \lnot bias(expensive), \allowbreak att_1,\allowbreak \lnot att_2, \allowbreak att_3,\allowbreak att_4,\allowbreak att_5, att_6\}$ 
            
    0.6::bias(hotel_X).
    0.8::bias(hotel_Y).
    0.4::bias(expensive).
    0.2::bias(noise).
    0.4::bias(anne).
    0.4::bias(week).
    0.6::bias(quiet).
    
    arg(X) :- bias(X).
    
    0.7::¬arg(hotel_Y) :- arg(hotel_X).
    0.4::¬arg(hotel_X) :- arg(hotel_Y).
    0.8::¬arg(hotel_Y) :- arg(noise).
    0.9::¬arg(hotel_X) :- arg(expensive).
    
    0.5::arg(hotel_Y) :- arg(anne).
    0.7::¬arg(noise) :- arg(week), arg(quiet). 
    
    evidence(arg(hotel_X)).
    query(arg(X)).
