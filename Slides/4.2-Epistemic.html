Epistemic approach

Epistemic approach: probabilities express a degree of belief over arguments.
  • The probability of an argument is fixed: if $>0.5$ then the argument is accepted.
  • Properties[1] define different types of probability assignments, for example:
    • Rational: for all arguments $a,b$ s.t. $(a,b)\in R$ and $P_R((a,b))>0.5$, $P_A(a)\geq 0.5$
      implies $ P_A(b)< 0.5$
    • Coherent: for all arguments $a,b$ s.t. $(a,b)\in R$ and $P_R((a,b))>0.5$, $P_A(a)\leq 1-P_A(b)$
  1. S. Polberg, A. Hunter, and M. Thimm, "Belief in Attacks in Epistemic Probabilistic Argumentation", 2017 Scalable Uncertainty Management
