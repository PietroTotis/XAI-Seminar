Constellations

Constellations approach: probabilities express uncertainty over the structure of the graph.
  • Possible worlds = all subgraphs induced by (independent) choices.
  • But some worlds/subgraphs include an attack but not the attacker1.
  • Probability of an argument = probability of being accepted w.r.t. a classical extension.
    $P(\omega) = \prod_{a\in \omega} P(a)\cdot \prod_{a\not\in \omega}1-P(a) $
    $P(arg) = \sum_{arg\in ext(\omega)} P(\omega)$
  1. T.Mantadelis, S.Bistarelli, "Probabilistic abstract argumentation frameworks, a possible world view", International Journal of Approximate Reasoning 2020.
