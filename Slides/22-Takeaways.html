Key Takeaways

  • Interpreting a probabilistic argument graph as a probabilistic graphical model compactly defines a joint probability distribution over arguments.
  • Modelling arguments in a joint probability distribution allows us to reason about degrees beliefs.
  • The joint probability distribution allows us to study the conditional dependencies between arguments, i.e. how they interact.
  • Using PLP to model the joint probability distribution allows us to apply the reasoning and learning tools of PLP (evidence, mpe, lfi, ...) frameworks (evidence, mpe, lfi, ...) on argumentation problems
