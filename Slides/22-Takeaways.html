Key Takeaways

  • Interpreting a probabilistic argument graph as a probabilistic graphical model compactly defines a joint probability distribution over arguments.
  • Modelling arguments in a joint probability distribution allows us to reason about degrees of belief.
  • The joint probability distribution allows us to study the conditional independencies between arguments, i.e. how their degree of belief interact.
  • Using PLP to model the joint probability distribution allows us to apply the reasoning and learning tools of PLP frameworks (evidence, mpe, lfi, ...) on argumentation problems
